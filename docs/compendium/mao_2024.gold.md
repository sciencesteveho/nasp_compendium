    nearest_existing: cytoplasmic_retroelement_cDNA
    why_insufficient: >
      cytoplasmic_retroelement_cDNA is reverse-transcribed retroelement DNA
      sensed by CGAS (the De Cecco/Martinez L1 branch). This node is RNA
      (double-stranded, from bidirectional ERV transcription) sensed by the
      RIG-I/MDA5-MAVS branch: a different molecular species feeding a different
      sensor. It parallels cytoplasmic_mt_dsRNA (mitochondrial dsRNA) in form but
      originates from retroelement loci, and it is the branch point that keeps
      the RNA-sensing arm distinct from the DNA-sensing arm while both share the
      upstream retrotransposon_derepression node.
    proposed_definition: >
      Cytoplasmic RNA, typically double-stranded from bidirectional transcription,
      derived from derepressed endogenous retroviruses or other retroelements and
      recognized by the cytosolic RNA sensors RIG-I (DDX58) and MDA5 (IFIH1).
    parallel_term_recommendation: >
      Promote as a symmetric pair with the DNA branch: generalize the existing
      canonical mechanism cytoplasmic_retroelement_cDNA to
      cytoplasmic_retroelement_DNA (covering reverse-transcribed cDNA and other
      retroelement-derived cytoplasmic DNA sensed by CGAS) and add a drift mapping
      cytoplasmic_retroelement_cDNA -> cytoplasmic_retroelement_DNA. This yields
      cytoplasmic_retroelement_RNA (-> RIG-I/MDA5) and cytoplasmic_retroelement_DNA
      (-> CGAS) on the shared retrotransposon_derepression node. The DNA-side
      rename touches agent/vocabulary.yaml plus the De Cecco and Martinez gold
      files and is left for human review.
