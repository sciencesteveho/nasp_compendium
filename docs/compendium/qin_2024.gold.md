    relevance_to_project: >
      A second cGAS-STING co-regulator alongside Lian/ZCCHC3, but acting at a
      different node: ZCCHC3 amplifies CGAS dsDNA binding upstream of cGAMP,
      whereas ZC3HAV1 amplifies STING trafficking and oligomerization downstream
      of cGAMP, so the two bracket the DNA-sensing spine. The sign-flipped RIG-I
      suppression makes ZC3HAV1 a regulator with opposite effects on the DNA
      (cGAS-STING, positive) and RNA (RIG-I/MDA5-MAVS, negative) arms, useful for
      modeling context-dependent regulator behavior. In atlas or perturbation
      data, treat ZC3HAV1 as a STING-axis amplifier whose level may set the gain
      of the cGAS-STING IRF3/NF-kB output, and use its node-specific localization
      to distinguish STING-proximal from CGAS-proximal modulators.
