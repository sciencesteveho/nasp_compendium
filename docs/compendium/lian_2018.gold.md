# Core co-sensor function: ZCCHC3 enhances CGAS dsDNA binding and oligomerization
# and is required for cGAMP synthesis; effect is CGAS-dependent and upstream of cGAMP.
  - chain_id: zcchc3_cosensor
    step: 2
    source: ZCCHC3
    target: CGAS
    rel: activates
    evidence_strength: perturbation_supported
    context: "ZCCHC3 itself binds dsDNA (poly(dA:dT), poly(dG:dC), HSV120) via its
              C-terminal zinc fingers but not ssDNA, and binds HSV-1 DNA at regions
              overlapping CGAS. ZCCHC3 overexpression enhances, and ZCCHC3
              knockout reduces, CGAS binding to dsDNA and CGAS oligomerization
              (SDD-AGE). By MST the ZCCHC3-CGAS complex binds dsDNA far more
              tightly (Kd ~7.6 nM) than CGAS alone (~600 nM). ZCCHC3 deficiency
              lowers HSV120-induced cGAMP. Epistasis: ZCCHC3 acts upstream of cGAMP
              (cGAMP-induced gene transcription is ZCCHC3-independent) and its
              effect on downstream genes is abolished in cGas-null L929 cells."
    support: "Fig. 4a,b; Fig. 5a-c; Fig. 6a-f; Fig. 7a-c"
    papers:
      - Lian_natcomms_2018
